**Jojoba Oil** (*Simmondsia chinensis* seed oil) is a liquid wax extracted from the seeds of the jojoba plant (*Simmondsia chinensis*), native to arid regions of North America.

## Healing Properties

### Antiproliferative

Jojoba oil exerts dose-dependent antiproliferative effects on nasal epithelial cells, reducing cell viability in a concentration-dependent manner as measured by the MTT colorimetric assay.[^1]

#### Cytotoxicity

Direct exposure of human nasal epithelial cells to jojoba oil results in a significant and progressive reduction in cellular metabolic activity across all tested concentrations (1, 5, 15, 25, 50, 75, and 100 µL per well over 24 hours). The estimated half-maximal inhibitory concentration (IC₅₀) was found to be 7.3 µL (logIC₅₀ = 0.8681), with a strong model fit (R² = 0.9055).[^1]

## Disease / Symptom Treatment

### Nasal Polyposis

The topical antiproliferative properties of jojoba oil may offer therapeutic benefit in the management of nasal polyposis.[^1]

### Mucosal Hyperplasia

Jojoba oil's concentration-dependent inhibitory effects on nasal epithelial cell proliferation suggest potential utility in treating mucosal hyperplasia.[^1]

### Granulation Tissue Formation

Topical application of jojoba oil may help manage excessive granulation tissue formation following nasal surgery.[^1]

### Skin Complications Following Rhinoplasty

The antiproliferative effects of jojoba oil on epithelial cells may be beneficial for addressing skin complications that arise after rhinoplasty.[^1]
